The 2st International Conference on Innovative Computing
(IC2017)
~Final Program~
OCU, Taichung, Taiwan || February 16-18, 2017

With recent advances in information technology, computer science and engineering, a comprehensive understanding to the essence of these realms and their derivatives becomes key and challenging issue to innovate future computing paradigms. The term, Innovative Computing (IC) - which covers a wide spectrum of computing, such as cloud computing, ubiquitous computing, green computing, service computing - seeks for all possibilities from these existing computing paradigms to craft new discoveries and applications that will shape the future.The aim of International Conference on Innovative Computing (IC conference) attempts to provide an open forum for sharing and discussion of state-of-the-art results covering all aspects of computer science and engineering. This conference is expected to bring together researchers and practitioners from both academia as well as industry to meet and share cutting-edge development in the field. Significant contributions in all major fields of the Computer Science and Information Technology in theoretical and practical aspects are highly welcome.

    • Author Guideline
    Original papers, Work-in-Progress and Demo/Poster addressing both theoretical and practical aspects are solicited. Submissions describing experience on real prototype implementations are particularly welcome. Papers should not be previously published or currently under review elsewhere. All submissions will be peer-reviewed and selected based on their originality and merit to the conference. Papers must be prepared at highest level of quality and prepared in compliance with the paper formatting template LNCS/LNEE format, available at here. Language: The official language for the conference is English. Less than satisfactory English usage may form the sole basis for rejection of a paper. Authors whose native language is not English are encouraged to re-check their papers by inviting native English speakers for revision or using English grammar checkers available.

    Page Limit: The maximum page limit is 12 pages including figures and tables, and 6 pages to Work-in-Progress and Demo/Poster papers.
